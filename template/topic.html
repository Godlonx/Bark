After that, it's up to you to decide what you need, but the good thing is that you can do a bit of what you want with these 7000s, gain a bit of performance for a bit more power consumption, have the best performance/watt ratio or go all out for ecowatt. My configuration: 7900xt sapphire, 5800x3d, 32go on a 3440/1440p @ 100Hz. 600w bequiet gold power supply. Personally, I haven't finished fine-tuning all this yet, but I'm sticking to 2450MHz max frequency for 1040mV voltage. VRAM set to 2714MHz (with the aim of being at 2700 battery in use) with memory latency on fast timer. I didn't touch the min frequency or the power limit. The fans are set to 50%. It's a Sapphire 7900XT. To compare, I must be losing 1/2% performance, and even then, at times it seems really insignificant. On the other hand, I've lost 70w in power consumption and the little coil whine (under load at 320w) when I put my ear next to the PC (I have a silent PC and a thin ear, for comparison) has disappeared. For information, my 5800x3d underwent a small -0.05v in the bios (-10W of consumption 5c less heating for no loss of performance). The cg also lost 5/6c of heat. Note, however, the controlled heating of the 5800x3d, around 40w IG. That's it! For those of you who own a 7900XT/XTX, don't hesitate to give it a go, it's clearly worth it! And don't forget to share your setup and results. EDIT: The games are full ultra, of course, except for cyberpunk in high.
